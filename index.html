Chris Quince

I am an internationally renowned expert in the bioinformatics and statistics of microbial community analysis from next generation sequence data. My research spans both bioinformatics for improved interpretation of sequence data and statistics for distilling ecological relevant information from the resulting high dimensional data sets. Common themes are the use of probabilistic models to address both types of problem and an abiding interest in microbial diversity, both how to detect it and its ecological relevance. I work closely with wet lab microbiologists and collaborate on projects spanning human health and the environment.
